{# ===================== 1.1 Mission ===================== #}
Our mission

Turn clinical needs into viable, collaborative AI projects.

The hub unlocks the potential of healthcare research and clinical data by lowering the barriers to AI innovation. Clinical partners and researchers submit their ideas; a multidisciplinary panel reviews them; and approved projects are published so that AI researchers across the university — from PhD students to academic staff — can collaborate.

Lower barriers to entry

Enable clinicians and healthcare researchers with no prior AI experience to pitch ideas and access advanced technical resources.

Foster cross-disciplinary collaboration

Connect frontline clinical knowledge (FoLSM) with the technical and deployment expertise within BMEIS.

Accelerate project delivery

Move ideas from a simple submission to active, fully-staffed research collaborations or hackathons.

Drive educational engagement

Identify skill gaps, host workshops, and help early-career researchers build new skills and networks.

{# ===================== 1.2 What the hub does ===================== #}
How it works

From a clinical idea to a launched project.

  1. 01

    Idea submission

    Clinical partners and researchers submit project ideas through an accessible form covering data, resources, timeline and ethics.

  2. 02

    Project review

    A multidisciplinary panel meets monthly to evaluate submissions on clinical impact, data readiness, resources and AI feasibility.

  3. 03

    Project publication

    Approved projects are published to an open platform, visible to researchers, developers and students looking to collaborate.

  4. 04

    Match & launch

    A PI is matched to coordinate the work, collaborators request to join, and the project is launched — or channelled into a hackathon.

Governance The hub is overseen by a monthly multidisciplinary review panel that evaluates every submission before it is published.

{# ===================== 1.4 Types of project ===================== #}
Ways to contribute

Types of project

Projects come in three effort bands, so anyone can contribute at the scale that fits.

Short-term

Hackathon

A focused sprint — days to weeks — to prototype an idea or explore a question at pace.

Medium-term

Proof of Concept

A few months to validate technical and clinical feasibility before a larger build.

Medium-term

Product Development

Building and hardening a deployable tool for real clinical or research use.

Long-term

Grant

A funded, long-term research programme with formal milestones and reporting.
